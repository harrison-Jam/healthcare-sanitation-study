# 🧼 Sanitation Practices in Public Healthcare Facilities in Ibadan

## 📊 SPSS Data Analysis Project

This project analyzes **sanitation practices in selected public healthcare facilities in Ibadan, Nigeria** using survey data and statistical analysis conducted in **IBM SPSS**.

The study evaluates sanitation infrastructure, waste management practices, hygiene behaviors, and the barriers affecting sanitation quality in healthcare environments.

---

# 📌 Project Objectives

The study was guided by the following objectives:

### 1. Evaluate Sanitation Facilities
To evaluate the **availability and functionality of sanitation facilities** such as toilets, wash basins, and waste disposal systems in selected healthcare facilities.

### 2. Examine Waste Management Practices
To examine **waste segregation, disposal, and treatment practices** of medical and non-medical waste within the facilities.

### 3. Assess Hygiene Practices
To assess the **hygiene practices of healthcare workers, patients, and visitors**, including adherence to national and international sanitation guidelines.

### 4. Identify Challenges and Barriers
To analyze the **challenges affecting proper sanitation** in healthcare facilities.

### 5. Provide Recommendations
To propose **practical recommendations for improving sanitation practices** in public healthcare facilities in Ibadan.

# 📊 Dataset Overview

- **Sample Size:** 227 respondents  
- **Participants:** Healthcare workers, patients, and visitors  
- **Location:** Public healthcare facilities in **Ibadan, Nigeria**

### Key Variables

- Facility cleanliness rating
- Toilet condition and cleaning frequency
- Handwashing accessibility
- Waste disposal infrastructure
- Waste bin emptying frequency
- Hygiene practices of healthcare workers
- Water supply availability
- Cleaning staff adequacy
- Sanitation resource availability

---

# 📈 Analysis Methods

Data analysis was conducted using **IBM SPSS** and involved:

- Frequency distributions
- Percentage analysis
- Descriptive statistics
- Data visualization (bar charts and tables)

The results were used to evaluate sanitation conditions and identify gaps in healthcare facility hygiene management.

---

# 🔎 Key Findings

Major findings from the study include:

- Most healthcare facilities were rated **clean**, though sanitation quality was inconsistent.
- Nearly **half of the facilities lack adequate waste disposal bins**.
- **Water supply was reported inadequate by over 50% of respondents**.
- **Cleaning staff shortages** were identified as a major sanitation barrier.
- Awareness of sanitation and waste disposal guidelines was **limited among many respondents**.

These findings highlight significant areas for improvement in healthcare sanitation systems.

---

# 💡 Recommendations

Based on the findings, the study recommends:

- Improving **water supply infrastructure**
- Increasing **cleaning staff availability**
- Providing **adequate waste disposal bins**
- Enhancing **training on sanitation and waste management guidelines**
- Strengthening **monitoring and supervision of sanitation practices**
